Untitled, 2019
These sculptures are representing, fysical powers such as gravity, tension, pressure and weight.

I made these sculptures with cilinders thrown on the potters wheel. In this way, they have an evenly wall, so there is no manipulation. 
The cilinders are then attached to eachother whith a squeeze between my thumb and index finger, which leaves a fine detail on the clay. 

By stocking them on top of eachother, there body transformes because of the tension between the cilinders, the pressure points and the gravity. They search for a way to escaped by searching al the empty spaces, with other words. the close the gaps between the cilinders.

I control the way I throw the cilinders on the wheel and how I place them in the sculpture, but I have no controle over how they will react to the fysical powers and themselfs.

It makes the sculpture interesting and very exiting to look at.
